Project #9 Data Portrait | Daily Laughter

Designed by Haoshu Yang.

Description

Laughter, one of the most common actions in our whole life, starts from the giggles when we are infants. It reveals the vividness in our normal life—the happy time, the leisure time, the grateful time, the releasing time, etc. Some of them blend into our memories and whenever we recall, they bring us to the similar moment. Yet, the others fade away. Therefore, I decided to document such moments and hope to find some unexpected pattern.

This work demonstrates the seconds of me laughing in 7 days from November 23 to November 29. The cyan half of the canvas indicates the day from 6:00 to 18:00 and the dark blue half indicates the night. 7 days of data scatters on the 7 concentric circles sequentially, where seconds in the day being illustrated as sun and those at night being illustrated as moon. If a moment is laughing together with friends, a halo is behind the icon. What’s more, if the content of making me laughing is conducted offline, in other world, I laugh because of things surrounding me in reality rather than those on the internet, the icon is connected to the center of the circles.

Design Process

After deciding to to visualize my laughing moments, I stared my ‘documentary’ 7 days. At the beginning, I was not used to writing down the time, the content and the level of laughter when I laughed. It was after hours that I remembered the task and recorded the approximate time. Later, I gradually formed a habit of recording the moments. Most of the documentations are recorded in real time, except for the 6th day’s midnight when we had an all-night party, played games and laughter never stopped, I threw myself into the party and added the moments the next day, with randomized level of laughter in the step of 10 minutes in different game time intervals.

Reflection

In computer graphics, images and patterns are all parameterized and could be generated from mathematics.

The forms of stars are protean due to the infinite values of outer and inner circle radiuses even in a limited range. The larger the difference of the radiuses is, the thinner the star point is. And with the increasing number of points, the star are more like a circle. Therefore, the point number is limited in a range of 3 to 8 for better illustration on this 400px*400px canvas.

The pattern of the trunk is also unpredictable, with 10px-vertical-length lines in increasing thickness joint together. Every line ends at the random position shifting 10px left or right horizontally. The extreme situation comes when every line is towards the same direction but the probability is less than 1/1000000000000000000000000000. Hence, almost all the time, we could see an arbitrary winding trunk.
